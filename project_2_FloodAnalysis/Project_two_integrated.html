Basic Statistical Analysis

The ten countries below account for 85% of the total number of floods deaths since 1985. We can notice that a) the countries mostly belong to the Southeast Asian region b) most of the floods deaths in Japan where caused by  tsunami c) Thailand is particularly vulnerable to tide surges d) Myanmar and Bangladesh have witnessed dangerous tropical cyclones and lastly e) India is particularly affected by heavy or tropical rain.

   Interpretation of Sankey plot 1:

In the left Sankey diagram we can see that most of the floods are due by heavy rain and they are equally distributed into the three categories: (<10,000 km^2  /   >10, 000 and <70,000 km^2  /  > 70,000 km^2). Besides, all the monsoonal rains come from Asia. What’s the most interesting here is to see the differences between those two plots. In the second plot Monsoonal rains have a far much higher weight than in the left plot. It means that even though most of the floods are caused by heavy rains they have usually no consequences on the people, they are not severe floods. On the contrary, monsoonal rains are far much violent and cause a lot of population movement of people who want to flee from the torrential rains. We can make the same observation for Tropical Cyclones. But sadly we will see that in the case of Tropical Cyclones it is the number of deaths that explodes.

The second thing to notice, is that even if there are a lot of floods in Europe, North America or in Australia, there is at the end very little population displacement. We can explain this observation by the fact that those countries are rich countries that have a lot of means to control the flood and avoid to displace people.

Interpretation of Sankey plot 2:

From the plot on the left we can see that for a given country, it has roughly equal numbers of short, middle or long flood. It is the same observation for every rain floods: they can occur with roughly the same odds in each category: short, middle or long flood. We can also notice that monsoonal rains last barely less than 4 days.

From the second plot, we can see that Asia is the country that has the biggest number of deaths because of floods. The most deadly floods are floods that last more than ten days. But surprisingly short floods are deadlier than middle floods. It is mainly because short flood are often associated to tide surges that are a really fast phenomenon but very violent and thus susceptible to cause a lot of deaths. We can see that tropical cyclones are the deadliest events, followed closely by Heavy rain and Tide surge.

The most interesting thing to notice here is the difference between the two plots. Tropical cyclones have a low weight on the left plot and a much bigger one on the right one. It means that tropical cyclones are rare events but when they happen they have terrible consequences. It is even worse for tide surges and tsunami.

We summarise the analysis above and present the information on the map. Most of the flood are caused by the heavy rain (evenly spread all over the world). The flood caused by the monsoonal rain and the snow are quite location specific while the floods caused by the snow are mostly located in Russia and Canada. The flood caused by the monsoonal rain are mostly located in the south east Asia. The flood caused by the tropical cyclone are mostly located at the board of continents.

        

The plot above give us an intuition on the distribution of duration, severity and casualty corresponding to each kind of flood, as well as their medians. The overall pictures tell us that the duration of floods is usually around 10 days, severity ranges from 1.0 to 1.25 and mean casualty is around 10 people. This informs that the distribution is skewed and most of the floods are not very destructive.

A closer look into different causes gives more ideas about their characteristics. As we can see, landslide and tsunami usually have a shorter duration, but they are destructive since they are high in severity and casualty. The casualty number stays about 1000, which is quite horrible. Flood by snow is usually high in severity, with median at 2.0, unlike other types of flood. The distribution tells us that different types of floods share some similarity in that they have median duration, low severity and median casualty.

Exploring relationship of Pressure and Flood Occurrences

We will now try to figure out if there is any correspondence between grid pressure values and the occurrence of floods in that area and generally if pressure variations are indicative of floods.

Empirical observation

The two animations compare pressure values when there was no flood and during the time of flood in Turkey. The first gif is when Turkey was flooded in the year 1992 (which resulted in the death of 200 people). One can notice a depression (blue region) appearing near Turkey.

The next animation is of the previous year when there was no flood. In this figure a high pressure blurb crosses Western Europe, Russia and hovers over Turkey.

Principal Component Analysis

To perform PCA, we take a slice of the 3d array and convert it into a 2d matrix. The rows are the grid points and the columns are the pressure values on each day since 2015-01-01. We also scale the matrix before performing the analysis.

The variance along each component is plotted in the above figure. As seen from the figure first 5-7 PC’s are the most useful in explaining a large chunk of variation.

Statistical Testing

Interpretation:

We took the floods dataset and we added the geopotential heights (Phi) values for each floods since 1985 (for floods over several days we took the mean). We created two new variables: Phi before the flood (Phi averaged over the week before the flood happened) and Phi after the flood (average value of the Phi’s 10 days after the flood ended). We can notice that this distribution is quite skewed to the right which indicates that usually the pressure after the flood is higher than the pressure just before the flood happened. Thus, floods seem to be triggered by depressions. To verify the correctness of our assumptions, we ran a t-test to verify if the mean of the difference in Phi is null or not. We had the following results:

The very low p-value confirms that we can say with high confidence that the pressure is always lower before a flood than after. Indeed, in a very simplistic point of view we can analyze that by saying that depressions triggers precipitations which then provoke floods.

The above analysis supports the empirical observation above where a region of depression formed above Turkey just before flooding.

Summary 

We cleaned the data, then looked at some basic exploratory data. For example, we looked at the relationship between the number of dead, number of displaced and flood severity. We found out that the number of dead and displaced increases with flood severity.

We then computed basic statistics. We studied the main floods causes for the top 10 deadliest countries in the world and discovered the weaknesses of each country. Then to go deeper into the data we plotted Sankey diagrams to show the conditional distributions of the number of floods (Sankey Diagram 1 and 3) or the number of displacements (Sankey 2) or the number of deaths (Sankey 4) conditioned on some floods features like: area affected, continent, duration of the flood, main cause of the flood… For example, we have seen that the type of floods that occur the most (heavy rain) are not the most dangerous in term of deaths or population displacement.

We found a definite correlation between the geopotential pressure values in the .nc file with the flood occurrences. We did an empirical analysis, PCA and statistical testing to confirm our hypothesis.

The news reports help us further investigate into different causes and different countries. A detailed case study on China, India, Japan and Thailand gives us more information on the location, time, types as well as other interesting points worth noticing. For example, in China, floods happened largely on July and August, caused by heavy rain and Typhoon in agricultural areas. In Japan, landslides and typhoon are common reasons and floods affect cities a lot other than agricultural areas. In Thailand, October and September are common flood seasons. In India, Monsoonal rain is the most common one. More word clouds are shown in Appendix part.

Observation:

 

1.         Both the duration time and the affected area of dam-malfunction caused flood is small, which is reasonable due to the artificial reason leading to the flood. In the flood caused by the dam malfunction, seldom people would be died but many of people would be displaced. This result shows that the government plays an important role in reducing the damage brought by the flood caused by the dam malfunction. The government could know the dam is out of function in the future several days or weeks, and then make the plan to displaced the people near the dam and reducing the number of people dead in the flood.

2.         The flood caused by the snow is long-time duration and affecting large-area of lands. But interestingly, both the number of people displaced and died in the snow-induced flood is quite small. It could be concluded that the snow-induced flood would continue a comparatively long term and large areas, but it is not so dangerous and would not arise much of attention from the government.

 

3.         The flood caused by the tropical cyclone is short-time duration and affecting less-area of lands comparing to the snow-induced flood. However, both the number of people displaced and died in the tropical-cyclone induced flood is quite large. It could be inferred that the tropical cyclone attacks quite sudden and it would have induced unexpected flood. According to this reason, a large number of people would be died in the short-period of time that the tropical cyclone continues. Though the tropical cyclone induced flood continue a comparatively short term and small areas comparing to the snow induced flood, but it is much more dangerous and the government should fully prepare the urgent plan to save the people in tropical cyclone induced-flood.

 

4.         In the flood caused by the tropical cyclone, the number of people displaced in North American is larger than that in Asia, but the number of people died in North American is smaller than that in Asia. We can infer that the government in developed country and developing country shows different ability in handling the flood and tropical cyclone.
